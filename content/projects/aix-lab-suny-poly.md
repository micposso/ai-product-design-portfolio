# AI Exploration Center (AIX) at SUNY Polytechnic Institute

## Summary

As part of my graduate experience at SUNY Polytechnic Institute, I joined the AIX Laboratory as a Graduate Technical Researcher, where I worked with Professor Dr. William Thistleton on research around edge AI and small language models that run on mobile devices. The work was supported through a New York State budget and connected open-source AI development with practical deployment and interface design.

## Context

The AI Exploration Center (AIX) at SUNY Polytechnic Institute is built around three connected programs: the Laboratory, the Accelerator, and the Studio. Together, they create a cycle between research, practical application, and user experience investigation.

The lab focuses on advancing AI models and theoretical knowledge. The accelerator bridges AI research into real-world organizational applications. The studio explores how people interact with AI systems and how those experiences can be designed more effectively.

## Challenge

Many AI systems assume cloud access, heavy compute, and highly technical users, which limits their usefulness in mobile, resource-constrained, and real-world environments where smaller, deployable systems are needed.

The challenge was to explore AI systems that are not only technically capable, but also practical to deploy, accessible to use, and relevant to real-world domains like agriculture and sustainability.

## What I Built

- Contributed to open-source AI application research focused on agriculture and sustainability.
- Worked on research around edge AI and small language models that run on mobile devices.
- Collaborated with Dr. William Thistleton inside the AIX Laboratory research environment.
- Contributed interface thinking through AIX Studio by helping develop open-source UI frameworks for AI-powered web and mobile applications.
- Connected technical AI research with UX and UI design considerations for more accessible tools.

## Discovery

The research began by exploring how SUNY Poly's AIX ecosystem could bridge theoretical AI work with practical deployment, especially in areas where lightweight models and edge-friendly systems matter more than scale alone.

## Build

Working with Dr. William Thistleton, I focused on open-source AI applications, edge AI experimentation, and small language models designed to run on mobile devices, while also contributing interface thinking through AIX Studio.

## Launch

The project connected technical research with an applied ecosystem of students, faculty, and partners, positioning the work as part of a broader effort to advance ethical AI, practical deployment, and more accessible AI-powered tools.

## Body

The AI Exploration Center, or AIX, at SUNY Polytechnic Institute is an action-driven research ecosystem focused on advancing AI innovation, promoting ethical AI development, and deepening user experience research. Its three-part structure, the Laboratory, the Accelerator, and the Studio, creates a useful model for connecting theory, application, and human interaction.

As part of my graduate experience at SUNY Poly, I joined the AIX Laboratory as a Graduate Technical Researcher. My work was supported through a budget granted by New York State, which gave me the opportunity to contribute to open-source AI applications focused on agriculture and sustainability while operating inside a broader research environment dedicated to practical AI development.

I worked with Professor Dr. William Thistleton to do research around edge AI and small language models that run on mobile devices. That direction came from a practical concern: many AI systems are optimized for cloud access, larger infrastructure, and heavyweight model assumptions, but those conditions do not reflect every real-world deployment environment.

This made edge AI and compact language models especially relevant. Smaller systems create opportunities for mobile-first AI tools, lower-cost deployments, and applications that can operate more realistically in constrained environments. That focus aligned closely with my broader interest in scalable AI for underserved contexts, especially where agriculture, sustainability, and accessibility intersect.

Alongside my work in the laboratory, I also collaborated with AIX Studio. There, I helped develop open-source UI frameworks for AI-powered web and mobile applications, drawing on my UX and UI design experience to create interfaces that are more intuitive, accessible, and user-centered.

What made the experience especially valuable was the overlap between technical AI research and interaction design. The laboratory created space to explore deployable systems and smaller models, while the studio emphasized how people actually engage with AI tools. That combination reinforced a research approach grounded in both implementation and usability.

The broader AIX ecosystem also shaped the work. The Laboratory supports model development and evaluation, the Accelerator focuses on turning theory into application, and the Studio examines participant behavior and human-AI interaction. Working within that structure made the research feel connected to real use cases instead of isolated from them.

For me, this project was not only a research appointment. It was a graduate-level step toward building AI systems that are open, practical, and deployable in realistic conditions. It also deepened my interest in edge computing, smaller models, sustainability-focused AI applications, and the interface systems that make these tools usable.

Looking back, the experience stands out because it combined research, open-source development, and design into one applied environment. It showed that meaningful AI work is not only about model scale. It is also about where systems can run, who can use them, and how responsibly they are introduced into practice.

## Outcome

The research helped define a practical direction for open-source AI applications focused on agriculture, sustainability, and accessible interaction design, while connecting technical experimentation in edge AI with broader work in UX, UI, and responsible deployment.
