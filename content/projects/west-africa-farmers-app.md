# Vision App For Farmers In West Africa

## Summary

A mobile concept that helps farmers in West Africa identify crop pests using edge vision models and a voice interface designed for local languages.

## Context

The project addresses a practical agricultural problem: helping farmers make faster decisions when crops show signs of damage. The design goal is to make AI useful in environments where connectivity, literacy, and language support all matter.

## What I Built

- Defined a mobile workflow for capturing crop images and running pest detection with edge vision models.
- Designed a voice layer so the app can explain results and next steps in local languages.
- Focused on accessibility and real-world usability for farmers who may benefit more from voice than text-heavy interfaces.
- Framed the project as an AI product that combines computer vision, edge inference, and multilingual voice interaction.

## Stack

Computer vision, edge models, mobile interaction design, multilingual voice UX, AI-assisted agriculture workflows

## Outcome

The project demonstrates how AI can be shaped into a more locally relevant and inclusive product experience. It combines technical feasibility with human-centered design for real-world use cases.
